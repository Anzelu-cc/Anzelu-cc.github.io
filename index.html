What Is Schizophrenia?

     

Schizophrenia is a severe mental health disorder that affects a person by how they think, feel, and behave. Some of the symptoms that come with it are hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, and having a hard time functioning every day. Even with the stereotypes around schizophrenia, it's not the same as having "split personality."

   
   
     

Common Misconceptions

     
           
  • Myth: "People that have schizophrenia are violent."
    Truth: Most individuals with schizophrenia aren't violent but are more likely to be a victims of violence than doing it.
    •        
  • Myth: "Schizophrenia is having multiple personalities."
    Truth: This is just completely wrong. People usually get this wrong because it comes from Dissociative Identity Disorder, which is something entirely different.
    •        
  • Myth: "Schizophrenia can't be treated."
    Truth: Surprisingly, despite what people think if someone with schizophrenia were given proper treatment, they can live a fulfilling life.
   
   
     

Signs and Symptoms

     
           
  • Hallucinations (seeing/hearing things that aren't there)
    •        
  • Delusions (false beliefs, usually paranoid)
    •        
  • Disorganized speech and behavior
    •        
  • Being emotionally flat or having a lack of motivation
    •        
  • Hard time thinking clearly or concentrating
    •      
   
   
     

Treatments

     
       

Medication: Medications are usually the most important part of treatment towards schizophrenia in handling symptoms and stopping relapses.

       

Therapy: Therapy can help with coping strategies and thought patterns.

       

Support systems: Family therapy, case management, and support groups are important in helping people diagnosed succeed in their communities.

       

Early intervention: Early treatment significantly improves long-term outcomes.

     
   
   
     

How you can help lower stigma

     
           
  • Be direct when you hear misinformation being said
    •        
  • Encourage people to be compassionate and understanding
    •        
  • Have yourself and others learn about schizophrenia
