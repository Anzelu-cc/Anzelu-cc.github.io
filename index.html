Schizophrenia

     

Schizophrenia is a mental health disorder that affects somehow and how they think, feel, and act. Some symptoms that come are hallucinations, delusions, confused thinking, and overall having a hard time going day by day. Even with all the stereotypes around schizophrenia, it's not the same as having "split personality," they are both two different things that should be separated but taken equally as seriously.

   
   
     

Mistakes when seeing schizophrenia

     
           
  • "People with schizophrenia are violent."
    Truth: Most people with schizophrenia aren't violent but are actually more likely to be a victim from violence than actually committing it.
    •        
  • "People with schizophrenia have multiple personalities."
    Truth: This is just completely wrong. The reason so many get this wrong is because it comes from Dissociative Identity Disorder, but that's something different entirely.
    •        
  • "Schizophrenia is something that can't be treated."
    Truth: Surprisingly, even though what people think if someone with schizophrenia, was given the right treatment, they'd be able to continue on with their lives normally.
   
   
     

Signs/symptoms

     
           
  • Hallucinations (seeing/hearing things that aren't there)
    •        
  • Delusions (false beliefs, usually paranoid)
    •        
  • Disorganized speech and behavior
    •        
  • Being emotionally flat or having a lack of motivation
    •        
  • Hard time thinking clearly or concentrating
    •      
   
   
     

Treatments

     
       

Medication: Medications are usually the most important part of treatment towards schizophrenia in handling symptoms and stopping relapses.

       

Therapy: Therapy can help with coping strategies and thought patterns.

       

Support systems: Family therapy, case management, and support groups are important in helping people diagnosed succeed in their communities.

       

Early intervention: Early treatment significantly improves long-term outcomes.

     
   
   
     

How you can help lower stigma

     
           
  • Be direct when you hear misinformation being said
    •        
  • Encourage people to be compassionate and understanding
    •        
  • Have yourself and others learn about schizophrenia
